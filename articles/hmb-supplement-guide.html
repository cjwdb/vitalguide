HMB Supplement Guide: Muscle Preservation, Dosing & Best Picks 2026

By the VitalGuide Editorial Team · April 2026 · 11 min read

HMB (beta-hydroxy-beta-methylbutyrate) is a metabolite of the essential amino acid leucine that has one of the most specific and well-documented mechanisms in sports nutrition: it suppresses muscle protein breakdown while simultaneously stimulating muscle protein synthesis. First patented and studied in the 1990s by Dr. Steven Nissen at Iowa State University, HMB saw early enthusiasm, a period of skepticism when initial results proved difficult to replicate, and a research renaissance in the 2010s–2020s as better-controlled studies confirmed its benefits — particularly for muscle preservation in older adults, injured athletes, and those in caloric deficit.

This guide explains exactly how HMB works, who benefits most, optimal dosing protocols, and the best supplements available in 2026.

What Is HMB and How Does It Work?

The Leucine-HMB Pathway

Leucine — the most anabolic essential amino acid — is metabolized in muscle and liver to alpha-ketoisocaproate (α-KIC), which is then converted to HMB. Only about 5% of dietary leucine is converted to HMB, meaning that obtaining meaningful amounts of HMB from food requires enormous leucine intake. Supplemental HMB bypasses this bottleneck, delivering a concentrated dose of the active metabolite.

Dual Mechanism: Anti-Catabolic + Anabolic

HMB works through two primary mechanisms:

  • Anti-catabolic: HMB inhibits the ubiquitin-proteasome proteolytic system — the primary pathway for muscle protein degradation. It specifically reduces the expression of muscle-specific E3 ubiquitin ligases (MuRF1 and Atrogin-1) that tag muscle proteins for degradation. This anti-catabolic effect is HMB's most distinctive and clinically significant property.
  • Anabolic: HMB activates the mTOR signaling pathway (the central regulator of muscle protein synthesis), though with less potency than high-dose leucine. It also appears to support satellite cell activity — the stem cells responsible for muscle repair and adaptation to training.

Membrane Stabilization

A third mechanism that distinguishes HMB: it serves as a precursor to cholesterol synthesis within muscle cells, contributing to cell membrane integrity. Following intensive exercise, cell membranes are damaged — HMB supplementation appears to accelerate membrane repair, reducing markers of muscle damage (creatine kinase, LDH) and supporting faster recovery. This mechanism explains why HMB's effects are most pronounced in untrained individuals, those returning from injury, and those beginning a new training program.

What the Research Shows

Muscle Preservation in Older Adults

This is HMB's most compelling and consistent evidence base. Multiple randomized controlled trials have shown that HMB supplementation (3 g/day) combined with protein intake and resistance exercise significantly attenuates age-related muscle loss (sarcopenia). A landmark study in Nutrition showed that HMB supplementation over 12 months in older adults who were not exercise training preserved lean mass and prevented functional decline. A follow-up trial found that HMB + Vitamin D + protein supplementation had additive effects on muscle mass preservation in institutionalized elderly. This evidence has made HMB a standard recommendation in geriatric nutrition guidelines for muscle preservation.

Injury and Immobilization

During periods of immobilization (cast, surgery, bed rest), muscle atrophy proceeds rapidly — at rates of 0.5–1% of muscle mass per day. Studies examining HMB during immobilization consistently show attenuated muscle loss compared to placebo. For athletes recovering from injury, HMB during the immobilization and early rehabilitation phase is one of the most evidence-backed interventions for muscle preservation.

Caloric Deficit / Fat Loss Phases

During caloric restriction for fat loss, muscle breakdown is elevated as the body catabolizes protein for gluconeogenesis. HMB's anti-catabolic mechanism is particularly valuable in this context — studies in athletes undergoing caloric restriction show HMB preservation of lean mass while fat loss proceeds, producing a more favorable body composition change than dieting alone. This makes HMB particularly relevant for physique athletes, combat sports athletes making weight, or anyone pursuing aggressive fat loss while preserving muscle.

Trained Athletes — More Modest Effects

In well-trained athletes without caloric restriction or injury, HMB's effects are more modest. The anti-catabolic effect is most impactful when muscle breakdown is elevated — in untrained individuals, older adults, those in caloric deficit, or those with injury. In chronically trained athletes in energy balance, HMB's incremental benefit over an already-optimized training and nutrition program is smaller. This explains the inconsistency in early HMB research — many initial studies used trained subjects in energy balance, where HMB's specific mechanism provides the least additional benefit.

HMB Forms: Free Acid vs. Calcium Salt

HMB is available in two forms:

  • HMB-Ca (calcium salt): The original, most studied form. Absorbed more slowly, with plasma peak at approximately 2 hours post-ingestion. This is the form used in most published research and has the longest safety track record.
  • HMB-FA (free acid): A newer form developed by Metabolic Technologies (the patent holder). Absorbed more rapidly (plasma peak at ~30–60 minutes), potentially making it more useful for pre-workout supplementation where the timing of peak plasma levels matters more. Some studies suggest HMB-FA produces greater acute exercise performance benefits, though the long-term data is less extensive than HMB-Ca.

For most applications (muscle preservation, recovery, older adults), HMB-Ca is the preferred form — it's the most studied and the safety data is comprehensive. For acute performance-oriented use (pre-workout), HMB-FA's faster absorption may provide an advantage.

HMB Dosing Protocol

The most studied dose is 3 grams per day, typically split into three 1 g servings with meals. This is the dose used in the majority of positive clinical trials and appears to be the effective maintenance dose.

  • Muscle preservation (injury, immobilization, older adults): 3 g/day divided with meals; continue throughout the recovery period
  • Caloric restriction / fat loss: 3 g/day; particularly valuable at this time as catabolism is elevated
  • Pre-workout performance (HMB-FA): 1–3 g approximately 30–60 minutes pre-exercise
  • Onset of benefits: Most studies show measurable effects within 2–4 weeks of consistent supplementation

HMB vs. Creatine: How They Compare

HMB and creatine are often compared as muscle-supporting supplements, but they work through fundamentally different mechanisms and serve different populations:

  • Creatine primarily enhances acute exercise performance (power output, strength) by regenerating ATP, and secondarily stimulates hypertrophy through increased training volume. It works best in trained athletes actively training hard.
  • HMB primarily prevents muscle breakdown and is most effective when catabolism is elevated — injury, immobilization, caloric deficit, aging. Its effects on exercise performance in trained athletes are smaller than creatine.

The two are complementary — creatine for performance enhancement, HMB for muscle preservation — and can be taken together without issue. For older adults, injured athletes, or those cutting calories, HMB provides targeted value that creatine does not fully replicate.

The Bottom Line

HMB is one of the few sports nutrition supplements with a well-characterized mechanism and consistent evidence in specific, high-value applications — muscle preservation in older adults, injury and immobilization recovery, and caloric restriction phases. It is not a universal performance supplement that benefits all athletes equally, but for the populations and contexts where it works, the evidence is genuine and clinically meaningful.

NOW Foods HMB provides the best value for daily supplementation; Optimum Nutrition HMB 1000 offers convenient dosing from a top-tier manufacturer; Metabolic Technologies provides the most research-backed pedigree. At 3 g/day consistently for at least 3–4 weeks, HMB's anti-catabolic effects become clinically apparent — making it a particularly valuable tool during the highest-risk periods for muscle loss.
