Tongkat Ali (Longjack): The Science-Backed Testosterone Herb Taking Over Biohacking in 2026

By the VitalGuide Editorial Team · April 2026 · 12 min read

Of all the natural testosterone-support compounds studied in the last two decades, Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) has accumulated among the most compelling clinical evidence — and yet it remains underappreciated outside of Southeast Asian traditional medicine and hardcore biohacking circles. That is changing fast. Tongkat Ali is now one of the fastest-growing supplement categories globally, driven by a wave of high-quality RCTs, high-profile endorsements from performance scientists, and a growing population of men seeking natural alternatives to TRT.

Known as "Longjack" in Western markets and Pasak Bumi in Indonesia, Tongkat Ali is a flowering tree native to the rainforests of Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, and Vietnam. Its roots have been used for centuries in traditional Southeast Asian medicine as an aphrodisiac, energy tonic, and treatment for male sexual dysfunction. What distinguishes it from most traditional aphrodisiacs, however, is the depth of its modern clinical validation — multiple double-blind, placebo-controlled RCTs across distinct populations have now demonstrated statistically significant effects on testosterone levels, sexual function, physical performance, and psychological stress markers.

This guide covers everything you need to know: the active compounds, the clinical evidence (including specific study details), how to choose a quality supplement, and the best Tongkat Ali products available in 2026.

What's Actually in Tongkat Ali?

The bioactivity of Tongkat Ali is attributed to several classes of unique phytochemicals found almost exclusively in Eurycoma longifolia:

  • Quassinoids (eurycomanone, eurycomalactone, eurycomanol): These are the signature bioactive compounds of Tongkat Ali and the subject of most mechanistic research. Eurycomanone is the most studied and is considered the primary marker of extract potency. Quassinoids appear to exert their testosterone-supporting effects through multiple pathways: inhibiting the enzyme aromatase (which converts testosterone to estrogen), inhibiting sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) activity (freeing bound testosterone), and stimulating Leydig cell activity in the testes to increase testosterone synthesis directly.
  • Eurypeptides (glycoproteins and small peptides): These low-molecular-weight peptides were identified in early animal studies as having significant aphrodisiac and energy-enhancing properties. They appear to work through a different mechanism than the quassinoids — modulating the hypothalamic-pituitary axis rather than acting directly at the testicular level — which may explain why whole-root extracts often outperform isolated quassinoid fractions in clinical studies.
  • Polysaccharides and mucopolysaccharides: These carbohydrate fractions have demonstrated significant immunomodulatory and anti-fatigue effects in both animal and human studies. The polysaccharide content also contributes to Tongkat Ali's ergogenic (performance-enhancing) properties.
  • Beta-carboline alkaloids (canthin-6-one, 9-methoxycanthin-6-one): These alkaloids have been studied for anti-tumor, anti-parasitic, and anti-inflammatory properties. They appear to contribute to the anti-stress and cortisol-modulating effects of Tongkat Ali.

Clinical Evidence: What the Research Actually Shows

1. Testosterone and Free Testosterone

The testosterone evidence for Tongkat Ali is among the most robust of any natural supplement. The landmark study in this area was a 2012 randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial published in the Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition by Talbott et al. The study enrolled 76 healthy men and women with moderate stress (confirmed by elevated salivary cortisol and testosterone-to-cortisol ratio) and randomized them to 200mg/day of Tongkat Ali water-soluble extract or placebo for 4 weeks. The results were striking: the Tongkat Ali group showed a 37% increase in free testosterone compared to baseline (vs. +15% in placebo, p<0.05), alongside a 16% decrease in serum cortisol and significant improvements in self-reported stress, anger, tension, and confusion.

A 2003 study in the British Journal of Sports Medicine by Hamzah and Yusof found that recreational male athletes taking Tongkat Ali extract (100mg/day) for 5 weeks while following a resistance training program showed significantly greater gains in lean muscle mass and muscular strength compared to the placebo group — consistent with the testosterone-elevating effects seen in other trials. The study also noted significantly increased testosterone levels in the Tongkat Ali group at weeks 3 and 5.

A 2014 pilot study in healthy elderly men and women (aged 57–72) published in Phytotherapy Research found that 200mg/day of Tongkat Ali extract for 5 weeks significantly increased total testosterone, free testosterone (by 122% in men), and DHEA concentrations versus placebo, with no adverse effects. This population is particularly relevant given the natural testosterone decline associated with aging.

2. Libido and Sexual Function

Tongkat Ali's reputation as an aphrodisiac has the most extensive traditional and modern evidence base of any single phytochemical. Multiple well-designed clinical studies have confirmed libido-enhancing effects in both men and women.

A 2021 double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT published in Andrologia enrolled 105 men with late-onset hypogonadism (low testosterone with symptoms) and randomized them to 200mg/day of standardized Tongkat Ali extract or placebo for 12 weeks. The Tongkat Ali group demonstrated statistically significant improvements in erectile function scores (IIEF questionnaire), sexual desire scores, and overall sexual satisfaction versus placebo, alongside significant increases in serum testosterone. The effect sizes on sexual function were clinically meaningful — comparable in magnitude to some pharmaceutical interventions at the lower dose range.

A 2010 study in the Asian Journal of Andrology examined Tongkat Ali's effects on men with low libido using a validated sexual desire inventory. After 12 weeks, men taking Tongkat Ali showed significantly greater improvement in libido scores than the placebo group, with the effect becoming significant by week 6. Notably, benefits were seen across all age groups in the study population (25–52 years), not just older men.

In women, a 2021 open-label study found that post-menopausal women reporting low sexual desire showed significant improvements in libido, energy, and mood scores after 12 weeks of Tongkat Ali supplementation — consistent with the free testosterone-elevating effects seen in mixed-gender studies.

3. Athletic Performance and Body Composition

The ergogenic effects of Tongkat Ali are increasingly recognized in sports performance literature. A 2013 randomized controlled trial in the Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition enrolled recreational resistance-training males and randomized them to 400mg/day Tongkat Ali extract or placebo for 12 weeks. The Tongkat Ali group showed significantly greater improvements in lean body mass (+0.5 kg net advantage vs. placebo), reduction in body fat percentage, and muscular endurance compared to placebo. These effects are consistent with the testosterone-elevating mechanism but also appear partly independent of it — suggesting the polysaccharide fractions contribute to direct anti-catabolic or anabolic signaling.

A 2014 study examining the effects of Tongkat Ali on physical performance in active seniors (aged 60+) found that participants taking 400mg/day for 5 weeks showed significant improvements in grip strength, walking speed, and scores on the "timed-up-and-go" physical function test compared to placebo — effects attributed to both testosterone support and direct muscle-sparing mechanisms.

For endurance athletes, preliminary evidence suggests Tongkat Ali may reduce post-exercise cortisol elevation and improve recovery. A crossover study in cyclists found reduced ratings of perceived exertion and faster recovery of muscle soreness in the Tongkat Ali condition compared to placebo.

4. Stress, Cortisol, and Mood

One of the most underappreciated aspects of Tongkat Ali's mechanism is its cortisol-modulating effect. Elevated cortisol suppresses testosterone synthesis by inhibiting GnRH and LH release, reduces sex drive, promotes muscle catabolism, and impairs sleep quality. Tongkat Ali appears to normalize cortisol through multiple mechanisms, including direct adrenal modulation and beta-carboline alkaloid activity.

The Talbott et al. 2012 RCT mentioned above showed a 16% reduction in salivary cortisol after 4 weeks of Tongkat Ali supplementation, alongside significant improvements in self-reported stress ratings across multiple validated psychological measures. These effects were observed in both men and women with moderate stress levels — making Tongkat Ali potentially useful as a general adaptogen in addition to its testosterone-specific benefits.

A 2016 clinical trial specifically examining Tongkat Ali in moderately stressed adults found significant improvements on the Profile of Mood States (POMS) questionnaire, with reductions in tension, anger, confusion, and fatigue scores after 4 weeks of supplementation. The stress-reducing effects appear robust enough to be clinically meaningful independent of the testosterone benefits.

5. Male Fertility

Several studies have examined Tongkat Ali's effects on sperm quality and male fertility. A 2010 study in Asian Journal of Andrology enrolled subfertile men with idiopathic infertility and found that 200mg/day of Tongkat Ali water extract for 9 months resulted in significant improvements in semen volume, sperm concentration, sperm motility, and sperm morphology. Spontaneous pregnancies were reported in 14.7% of the treated group versus 5.6% in the control group — a clinically meaningful difference.

The mechanism here is likely multifactorial: direct stimulation of spermatogenesis in the testes (via LH-stimulated testosterone increase), reduction of oxidative stress in seminal plasma (antioxidant properties of quassinoids), and the direct effects of testosterone on sperm maturation.

Who Should Consider Tongkat Ali?

  • Men with low or declining testosterone — particularly those 30+ experiencing fatigue, reduced libido, or decreased muscle mass; the RCT evidence for testosterone support is the strongest in any natural testosterone category
  • Men and women under chronic stress — the cortisol-lowering effect makes Tongkat Ali effective as an adaptogen even for those without testosterone deficiency
  • Athletes and physically active individuals — the lean mass, strength, and recovery data supports use as a performance supplement
  • Men concerned about sexual health — the libido and erectile function evidence is robust and well-replicated
  • Couples trying to conceive — the male fertility data is compelling, particularly for unexplained subfertility
  • Active older adults (50+) — the performance and testosterone benefits appear especially pronounced in aging populations with naturally declining testosterone

How to Choose a Quality Tongkat Ali Supplement

Tongkat Ali quality varies dramatically between products, and the supplement market is unfortunately flooded with underdosed, mislabeled, and adulterated products. Here is what separates a genuinely effective product from the majority:

  • Extract ratio vs. standardized extract: Most effective Tongkat Ali research has used water-soluble root extracts (not whole root powder). Look for products standardized to eurycomanone content (the primary bioactive quassinoid), ideally ≥1% eurycomanone. A "200:1 extract ratio" label is meaningless without bioactive standardization — it tells you nothing about potency.
  • Clinical dose: Most positive RCTs used 200–400mg/day of standardized extract. Many products underdose at 100mg or combine tiny amounts with proprietary blends. Verify the per-dose Tongkat Ali content is at least 200mg of standardized extract.
  • Malaysian vs. Indonesian sourcing: Malaysian Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) is the most studied variety and the source of virtually all clinical research. Some products use Indonesian or Thai varieties that have not been comparably studied. Malaysian sourcing is preferable, with the gold standard being the Malaysian government's certified Physta® extract.
  • Third-party testing: Due to heavy metal concerns (root extracts can concentrate soil contaminants) and the prevalence of adulteration, choose products with third-party certificates of analysis.
  • Physta® certification: The most clinically validated standardized extract is Physta® (developed by the Malaysian government's Forest Research Institute Malaysia), used in over a dozen peer-reviewed human trials. Products using Physta® extract will be explicitly labeled.

Dosing and How to Take Tongkat Ali

  • Effective dose: Most RCTs demonstrating testosterone and libido benefits used 200–400mg/day of standardized extract. The stress and cortisol studies used 200mg/day. For athletic performance and body composition studies, 400mg/day was more common. Start at 200mg and increase to 400mg after 4–6 weeks if needed.
  • Timing: Most studies used once-daily morning dosing. Some practitioners recommend taking Tongkat Ali in the morning to align with the natural testosterone peak (early morning hours). Avoid evening dosing as the energizing effects may occasionally interfere with sleep in sensitive individuals.
  • Cycling: The traditional and most commonly recommended approach is a 5-days-on, 2-days-off weekly cycle, or an 8-weeks-on, 2-weeks-off cycle. There is limited long-term safety data beyond 12 months of continuous use; cycling is a precautionary measure rather than an evidence-based requirement. Many users take Tongkat Ali continuously without reported issues.
  • With food or fasted: Can be taken with or without food. Some evidence suggests slightly better absorption when taken on an empty stomach or with a small amount of fat (lipophilic compounds in the extract may benefit from dietary fat).
  • Stacking: Tongkat Ali pairs well with other testosterone-support supplements. Common evidence-backed combinations include Tongkat Ali + zinc (essential cofactor for testosterone synthesis), Tongkat Ali + ashwagandha (complementary cortisol-lowering and testosterone-supporting mechanisms), and Tongkat Ali + fadogia agrestis (LH-stimulating effect, though fadogia has more limited human data).

Safety and Precautions

  • Generally well-tolerated: All published human RCTs have reported Tongkat Ali as well-tolerated with no significant adverse events at doses up to 400mg/day for up to 12 months. No liver toxicity signals have emerged in the clinical literature despite early concern from case reports involving adulterated products.
  • Mercury contamination risk: Several cases of mercury toxicity attributed to "Tongkat Ali" supplements were later found to involve adulterated products (not genuine Eurycoma longifolia) or root extracts from unregulated sources. This makes third-party heavy metal testing non-negotiable — choose certified products from reputable brands.
  • Hormone-sensitive conditions: Because Tongkat Ali elevates testosterone, individuals with hormone-sensitive cancers (prostate, testicular) should avoid use. Consult an oncologist before use if you have a relevant cancer history.
  • Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH): Higher testosterone levels can theoretically worsen BPH symptoms in some individuals. Men with diagnosed BPH should consult a urologist before starting Tongkat Ali.
  • Drug interactions: Limited data exists on drug interactions. Theoretical interactions include blood thinners (anticoagulants), immunosuppressants, and medications metabolized by CYP3A4. Consult your physician if you take prescription medications.
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding: Safety has not been established — avoid during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
  • Children and adolescents: Not appropriate for individuals under 18 years of age.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly does Tongkat Ali work?

Most clinical studies show measurable testosterone and cortisol changes at 4 weeks, with libido and energy effects often reported anecdotally within 2–3 weeks. The athletic performance and body composition studies show meaningful effects at 5–12 weeks. Tongkat Ali is not a stimulant — it does not produce acute effects. The benefits build gradually as the hormonal environment shifts over weeks of consistent use.

Is Tongkat Ali the same as fadogia agrestis?

No — they are entirely different plants with different mechanisms. Fadogia agrestis (Fadogia agrestis) is a West African shrub that gained popularity after Andrew Huberman discussed it publicly. It appears to raise luteinizing hormone (LH) independently of the hypothalamic-pituitary axis. Tongkat Ali works through multiple mechanisms including quassinoid-mediated testosterone synthesis stimulation, SHBG inhibition, and aromatase inhibition. Tongkat Ali has significantly more human RCT data than fadogia agrestis. Many users stack both, but they are distinct supplements.

Can women take Tongkat Ali?

Yes, and it appears effective in women too. Women produce testosterone in small but physiologically important quantities, and declining testosterone with age (especially post-menopause) is associated with reduced libido, energy, and muscle mass. Studies including women have shown benefits in libido, energy, mood, and stress markers. The testosterone-elevating effects in women are smaller in absolute terms than in men but are within safe physiological ranges at 200mg/day. Women should start at 100–200mg/day and monitor for androgenic side effects (acne, increased body hair) at higher doses.

Will Tongkat Ali show up on a drug test?

Tongkat Ali is not a prohibited substance in most sports organizations, including WADA. It is not a synthetic anabolic steroid and does not directly introduce exogenous hormones. However, it may raise endogenous testosterone levels, and some sports organizations monitor testosterone-to-epitestosterone ratios. If you are a competitive athlete subject to drug testing, consult your sport's governing body about natural testosterone-support supplements before use.

Does Tongkat Ali work for men with normal testosterone?

The evidence is strongest for men with low-normal or below-normal testosterone, but some studies have shown benefits in healthy eugonadal men as well — particularly for libido, mood, and stress markers. The testosterone elevation appears to have a dose-response relationship with baseline levels: men with lower starting testosterone see larger absolute gains. Men with already-high testosterone may see smaller effects on hormone levels but can still benefit from the cortisol-lowering and adaptogenic properties.

Tongkat Ali is, without question, one of the best-evidenced natural testosterone-support compounds available. Its clinical track record — across testosterone, free testosterone, libido, athletic performance, cortisol, and male fertility — is deeper and more rigorous than virtually any competitor supplement in the space. For men experiencing the natural testosterone decline of aging, or anyone dealing with stress-driven hormonal suppression, it represents a meaningful, evidence-based intervention.

The key is choosing a high-quality product. Prioritize supplements using Physta®-certified extract (Akarali) or eurycomanone-standardized extracts with verified COAs (Nootropics Depot). When in doubt about interactions with existing health conditions or medications, consult your physician before starting.
