Tongkat Ali: What the Science Actually Says About Testosterone, Energy & Performance

By the VitalGuide Editorial Team · Last Updated: April 2026 · 10 min read

Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) has graduated from obscure Southeast Asian folk remedy to one of the most studied herbal testosterone modulators on the market. Used for centuries in Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand for energy, libido, and male vitality, it's now backed by over 30 clinical trials and a growing body of mechanistic research. But like most supplements, the marketing often outpaces the evidence. This guide covers what Tongkat Ali actually does, the dose that works, and the best products available.

What Is Tongkat Ali?

Tongkat Ali is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia — primarily Malaysia and Indonesia. Its name translates loosely to "Ali's walking stick" in Malay, a nod to its traditional association with male vitality. The active compounds are primarily quassinoids (eurycomanone, eurycomanol) and other alkaloids concentrated in the root.

It's sometimes called Longjack, Malaysian Ginseng, or Pasak Bumi. For quality research purposes, look for products standardized to 1–2% eurycomanone content, or that specify a clinically-tested extract such as Physta®, LJ100®, or Tongkat Ali 200:1.

How Tongkat Ali Works: The Mechanisms

Tongkat Ali doesn't produce testosterone — it modulates the hormonal environment that determines how much free testosterone circulates in your body. The key mechanisms:

  • SHBG inhibition: Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG) binds to testosterone and renders it biologically inactive. Eurycomanone and other quassinoids in Tongkat Ali appear to reduce SHBG activity, increasing the free-to-total testosterone ratio. This is clinically significant — free testosterone is what your tissues actually use.
  • LH stimulation: Tongkat Ali appears to stimulate the release of Luteinizing Hormone (LH) from the pituitary, which signals the testes to produce more testosterone. This is distinct from testosterone replacement therapy, which works by supplying exogenous testosterone and suppresses LH.
  • Cortisol modulation: Chronic stress and elevated cortisol directly suppress testosterone. Several Tongkat Ali trials show significant reductions in cortisol — which may explain part of its testosterone-elevating effect.
  • Phosphodiesterase inhibition: Some quassinoids inhibit PDE (phosphodiesterase), similar to the mechanism of certain erectile dysfunction medications, which may contribute to improved blood flow and libido.

What the Research Shows: Testosterone

The testosterone research on Tongkat Ali is more robust than for most herbal supplements. Key findings:

A 2012 randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial published in Evidence-Based Complementary and Alternative Medicine gave 200 mg/day of a standardized Tongkat Ali extract (Physta®) to 76 men and women with moderate stress. After 4 weeks, total testosterone increased 37% in men (from 385 to 527 ng/dL), free testosterone increased significantly in both men and women, and cortisol decreased 16%.

A 2014 pilot study in aging men (ages 57–72) gave 400 mg/day of a Tongkat Ali extract for 5 weeks. Total testosterone rose from 365 to 485 ng/dL (33% increase) and free testosterone also increased significantly. DHEA, a testosterone precursor, also rose meaningfully.

A 2021 meta-analysis in Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition analyzed 11 studies and concluded that Tongkat Ali supplementation produced statistically significant increases in both total and free testosterone, with stronger effects in men with low-normal or stress-suppressed baseline levels.

Important caveat: Effects appear most pronounced in men with subnormal testosterone (below 400 ng/dL), chronic stress, or who are aging (40+). Men with already-optimal testosterone levels see smaller absolute gains.

What the Research Shows: Libido and Sexual Function

Multiple trials document improvements in libido and erectile function with Tongkat Ali supplementation:

  • A 12-week trial in men with late-onset hypogonadism found improvements in the Aging Males' Symptoms (AMS) questionnaire, with libido and sexual performance subscores improving most substantially.
  • A 2017 study in infertile men showed improvements in semen volume, sperm motility, and sperm concentration after 3 months of supplementation — potentially relevant for couples trying to conceive.
  • Animal studies consistently show pro-erectile and libido-enhancing effects, with mechanisms overlapping PDE inhibition and testosterone signaling.

What the Research Shows: Athletic Performance and Body Composition

The ergogenic (performance-enhancing) evidence is promising but more preliminary:

  • A 5-week resistance training study gave cyclists 100 mg/day of Tongkat Ali. The supplemented group showed significantly greater arm circumference increases and improved cycling ergometer performance compared to placebo.
  • A 2003 study in recreationally trained men found that Tongkat Ali supplementation combined with resistance training produced greater increases in lean mass and strength compared to training alone.
  • A 2014 double-blind RCT gave 400 mg/day to physically active men for 5 weeks. The Tongkat Ali group showed significantly lower muscle soreness and faster recovery from eccentric exercise.

The athletic performance data is not conclusive enough to position Tongkat Ali as a primary ergogenic aid, but it works synergistically with a structured training program — particularly for men experiencing stress-related testosterone suppression.

What the Research Shows: Stress and Mood

One of the most consistently documented effects is cortisol reduction. The stress-testosterone relationship is bidirectional — chronic cortisol elevation suppresses testosterone, and testosterone supports mood and stress resilience. Tongkat Ali appears to intervene at both ends.

A 4-week study in moderately stressed adults showed reductions in tension, anger, and confusion on the Profile of Mood States (POMS) questionnaire, alongside the cortisol and testosterone improvements noted above. This makes it particularly relevant for men experiencing what's sometimes called "functional hypogonadism" — low testosterone driven by chronic stress rather than primary testicular failure.

Tongkat Ali Dosage: What Works

The clinical literature clusters around two effective dose ranges:

  • 200 mg/day: The most common dose in trials. Works for most general health and hormone support applications. Use a standardized extract (Physta® or equivalent).
  • 400 mg/day: Used in several aging male studies and for more pronounced effects on libido and athletic performance. Some men find this dose more effective after 4–6 weeks.
  • Timing: Most studies dosed once daily in the morning. Taking with food is recommended to reduce any GI sensitivity.
  • Duration: Clinical effects take 4–8 weeks to fully manifest. Short-term use (1–2 weeks) will not produce measurable hormonal changes.

Cycling is not required. Unlike synthetic testosterone or pro-hormones, Tongkat Ali works through endogenous hormone pathways and does not suppress natural production. Continuous use is appropriate.

Who Should Consider Tongkat Ali?

Tongkat Ali is best suited for:

  • Men 35+ experiencing low energy or libido: Testosterone naturally declines ~1–2% per year after 30. Tongkat Ali is most effective in this population.
  • Men under chronic stress: Cortisol suppression is a consistent finding. If your low energy is stress-driven, Tongkat Ali may help break the cortisol-testosterone cycle.
  • Athletes seeking natural hormone optimization: It's not a steroid, but the evidence supports meaningful improvements in free testosterone and recovery when training is consistent.
  • Men with borderline testosterone (300–450 ng/dL): The absolute gains are larger in men with low-normal testosterone than in men who are already optimal.

Safety and Side Effects

Tongkat Ali has a good safety profile in clinical doses. No serious adverse effects have been reported in human trials using 200–400 mg/day of standardized extracts. Mild side effects occasionally noted include:

  • Mild GI discomfort (reduced by taking with food)
  • Restlessness or sleep disruption at higher doses (take in the morning)
  • Mild insomnia in sensitive individuals

Important: Mercury contamination has been found in some low-quality Tongkat Ali products. Always buy from brands with third-party heavy metal testing. This is particularly important because the root comes from regions where soil contamination varies widely.

Not recommended during pregnancy. If you are on testosterone replacement therapy or other hormone medications, consult your physician before adding Tongkat Ali.

The Bottom Line

Tongkat Ali is one of the few herbal supplements where the evidence for testosterone support is actually compelling — not just anecdotal. The mechanism is biologically plausible, the human trials are well-designed, and the effect sizes are meaningful for the target population. It's not a replacement for lifestyle foundations (sleep, resistance training, stress management, healthy diet), but it can meaningfully optimize testosterone within the context of a healthy lifestyle.

Choose a standardized extract (200–400 mg/day), buy from brands with third-party testing, and give it 6–8 weeks before evaluating results. Track your energy, libido, mood, and recovery — these subjective markers often move first, before any lab confirmation.
