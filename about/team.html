Our Editorial Process

Every VitalGuide article follows the same evidence-based review process before publication:

1. Primary research review

We start with PubMed, Google Scholar, and Cochrane for systematic reviews and randomized controlled trials. We do not base claims on review articles, influencer content, or brand white papers.

2. Dose accuracy check

Supplement recommendations reference the doses actually studied in clinical trials — not label doses optimized for manufacturing costs.

3. Form specificity

Where form matters (magnesium glycinate vs. oxide, D3 vs. D2, methylfolate vs. folic acid), we specify the clinically relevant form and explain why.

4. Safety and contraindications

We disclose known contraindications, drug interactions, and population-specific risks. We do not minimize safety concerns to make a product easier to recommend.

5. Affiliate independence

Product selections are based on formulation quality and clinical evidence. Commission rates do not influence rankings. We disclose all affiliate relationships on every article.
