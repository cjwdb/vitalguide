# Sports Nutrition — Fueling Performance and Recovery

## Introduction

Sports nutrition has a reputation for excess — tubs of powder, lists of unpronounceable ingredients, and promises calibrated more for marketing than for science. But the core of what actually works is considerably simpler: adequate protein for muscle repair and synthesis, carbohydrates and electrolytes to sustain output and recover from it, and a handful of well-researched compounds that genuinely support performance at the margins.

The products that stand the test of time in this space are the ones grounded in straightforward nutritional science. They provide what the body needs, in effective doses, without unnecessary additives. That philosophy applies whether you're a competitive athlete or someone who trains four days a week and wants to feel good doing it.

The five products below represent some of the most trusted names across hydration, protein, creatine, collagen, and plant-based options — covering the key areas where thoughtful supplementation has the clearest evidence behind it.

Vital Proteins Collagen Peptides dissolves completely in hot or cold liquids with no taste or texture, making it easy to add to coffee, smoothies, or any meal. It's a practical daily addition for anyone doing high-volume training, recovering from a connective tissue injury, or simply trying to support joint health over time.

## How to Choose

Match your sports nutrition stack to your actual training demands. Hydration and electrolytes matter most when sessions are long, intense, or in hot conditions. Protein timing (within a couple hours of training) supports muscle protein synthesis, and total daily protein intake matters more than any specific timing window. Creatine works best with consistent daily use regardless of whether you take it pre- or post-workout. Collagen is best consumed with vitamin C about 30–60 minutes before training for connective tissue benefit. Start with the basics — adequate protein and hydration — before adding more targeted compounds.
