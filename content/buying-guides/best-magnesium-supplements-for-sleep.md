## Why Magnesium Helps You Sleep

Magnesium is involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body, including those that regulate the production of melatonin—the hormone that controls your sleep-wake cycle. It also activates the parasympathetic nervous system (the "rest and digest" state), calms the brain by binding to GABA receptors, and helps lower cortisol levels.

Studies published in the *Journal of Research in Medical Sciences* found that older adults who supplemented with magnesium fell asleep faster, slept longer, and reported significantly better sleep quality compared to those taking a placebo.

Not all magnesium is created equal. Here are the main types relevant to sleep:

- **Magnesium Glycinate** – Highly bioavailable, gentle on the stomach, excellent for sleep and anxiety
- **Magnesium L-Threonate** – Crosses the blood-brain barrier; best for cognitive and sleep benefits
- **Magnesium Citrate** – Affordable and effective, but may have a mild laxative effect at high doses
- **Magnesium Malate** – Better for energy and muscle recovery, less ideal specifically for sleep

For sleep, **magnesium glycinate** and **magnesium L-threonate** are the top choices.

## How to Take Magnesium for Sleep

**Timing:** Take magnesium 30–60 minutes before bed for best sleep results.

**Dosage:** The Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) for magnesium is 310–420 mg for adults, but therapeutic doses for sleep typically range from 200–400 mg of elemental magnesium.

**Starting slow:** If you're new to magnesium supplements, start at 100–200 mg and gradually increase to minimize any gastrointestinal adjustment.

**Consistency:** Magnesium works best with consistent daily use rather than as a one-time sleep aid. Most people notice improvements within 1–2 weeks.

**Stacking:** Magnesium pairs well with L-theanine, melatonin (low dose: 0.5–1 mg), and ashwagandha for a comprehensive sleep support stack.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is magnesium safe to take every night?**
Yes, for most healthy adults. At doses below 350 mg of supplemental magnesium (not counting dietary intake), it's considered safe by the NIH. Consult your doctor if you have kidney disease, as the kidneys regulate magnesium excretion.

**How long does magnesium take to work for sleep?**
Many people notice improved sleep within a few days to a week of consistent use. For those with significant deficiency, it may take 2–4 weeks to see full benefits.

**Can I take magnesium with melatonin?**
Yes. Magnesium and melatonin have complementary mechanisms and are commonly taken together. Use a low melatonin dose (0.5–1 mg) for best results.

**What's the difference between magnesium glycinate and oxide?**
Magnesium oxide has very poor bioavailability (around 4%) and is primarily used as a laxative. Magnesium glycinate is approximately 80% absorbed and is the preferred form for sleep and anxiety.
