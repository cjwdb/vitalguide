# Best Weighted Blankets for Adults in 2024: Complete Buying Guide

Few sleep accessories have exploded in popularity as quickly as the weighted blanket. What was once a therapeutic tool used by occupational therapists for sensory processing disorders has become a mainstream sleep essential—and for good reason. The gentle, distributed pressure a weighted blanket provides mimics the calming effect of a deep-pressure hug, activating the parasympathetic nervous system and promoting the release of serotonin and melatonin.

Whether you struggle with anxiety, insomnia, restless leg syndrome, or just want a cozier sleep experience, this guide covers the **best weighted blankets for adults** across every budget and body type.

## How Weighted Blankets Work

Weighted blankets use a therapeutic technique called **Deep Pressure Stimulation (DPS)**—the same principle behind swaddling infants, thunder shirts for anxious pets, and compression clothing for athletes. The firm, even pressure across the body signals the nervous system to shift from a sympathetic ("fight or flight") to parasympathetic ("rest and digest") state.

Research published in the *Journal of Sleep Medicine & Disorders* found that 63% of participants reported lower anxiety after using a weighted blanket, and 78% said they preferred sleeping with one for a better, calmer night's rest.

**Key physiological effects:**
- Increases serotonin production (precursor to melatonin)
- Lowers cortisol (the primary stress hormone)
- Reduces heart rate and promotes relaxation
- Provides proprioceptive input that helps regulate sensory processing

## Choosing the Right Weight

The most important variable in selecting a weighted blanket is the weight itself. The standard recommendation from occupational therapists is **10% of your body weight**. However, this is a starting guideline, not a hard rule:

| Body Weight | Recommended Blanket Weight |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| 100–150 lbs | 10–15 lbs |
| 150–200 lbs | 15–20 lbs |
| 200–250 lbs | 20–25 lbs |
| 250+ lbs | 25 lbs or custom |

**Other weight considerations:**
- People with anxiety often prefer slightly heavier blankets (12% of body weight)
- People who feel claustrophobic should start lighter (8% of body weight)
- Hot sleepers should prioritize breathable materials over maximum weight
- Couples who want to share a blanket should choose based on the lighter partner

---

## What to Look for When Buying

**Filler type:** Glass beads are quieter, denser, and stay in place better than plastic poly pellets. For a premium experience, choose glass beads.

**Fabric cover:** Options include minky/plush (warm and soft), cotton (breathable and washable), bamboo (cooling and hypoallergenic), and cooling fabric (often polyester blends with moisture-wicking properties).

**Construction:** Look for small internal pockets (2"x2" or similar) that prevent beads from shifting. More sections = more even weight distribution.

**Washability:** Can it be washed at home? Check if your washing machine can handle the weight (most home machines handle up to 15–18 lbs; heavier blankets may need a commercial machine).

**Size:** A weighted blanket should cover you but not hang heavily off the sides of the bed, as this can cause it to pull and feel uneven.

## Weighted Blanket Care Tips

**Washing:** Use cold water on a gentle cycle. Most blankets under 15 lbs can be washed in a standard home machine. For 20+ lbs, use a commercial machine at your local laundromat.

**Drying:** Tumble dry low or air dry. High heat can damage bead pockets and stitching over time.

**Storage:** Store folded or rolled—never compressed in a small space for extended periods, as this can stress the stitching around bead pockets.

**Frequency:** Wash every 2–4 weeks with regular use, or immediately if soiled. Many users keep a removable cover on their blanket to extend time between full washes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can weighted blankets help with anxiety?**
Yes. Multiple studies support the use of weighted blankets for anxiety reduction. The deep pressure stimulation activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing physiological stress responses.

**Is a 20 lb weighted blanket too heavy?**
For adults weighing 150–200 lbs, a 20 lb blanket is appropriate. For lighter adults or first-time users, starting with 15 lbs is recommended.

**Can I use a weighted blanket if I'm claustrophobic?**
Yes, but start with a lighter weight (8–10% of body weight) and a breathable material like cotton or Bearaby's knit style. Many people with claustrophobia adapt well to lighter weighted blankets.

**Are weighted blankets safe to sleep under all night?**
For healthy adults, yes. Weighted blankets should not be used by infants, toddlers under 2, or individuals with respiratory conditions, claustrophobia (without consultation), or limited mobility without medical guidance.
